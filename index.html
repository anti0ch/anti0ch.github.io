Cosmo Chou

I am a biomechanics researcher with expertise in prosthesis design, control, and instrumentation; orthosis design and instrumentation; bio-inspired and soft robotics; inertial sensing; sensor fusion; gait dynamics and kinematics; and machine learning-aided stance prediction.

I am a graduate student in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Alabama, Tuscaloosa working under my advisor Dr. Xiangrong Shen in the Human-Inspired Biorobotics Lab and with Dr. Vishesh Vikas in the Agile Robotics Lab. Currently, I work on the design and control of lower-body mobility assistive devices, developing wearable instrumentation for gait biomechanic capture, and recognition/classification of stance-to-stance transitions.

Research Interests

Prosthesis Design

My research focuses on the design, fabrication, and control of artificial knees in transfemoral prostheses.

Gait Biomechanics

By better understanding and quantifying the behavior of the biological leg, artificial replacements can be improved.

Nonlinear Stability Theory

Poincare mapping and other techniques for assessing dynamic stability.

Fabricating Anisotropy

Manufacturing and using anisotropic components to eliminate directional noise.

Predicting Stance Transitions

Using Bayesian classification to anticipate walking to stair climbing, sit to stand, and other gait or stance transitions.
